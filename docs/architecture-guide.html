How the Narrative System Works

A plain-language guide to what's running behind the scenes when you're writing stories

First, how SillyTavern works

The Old Way: SillyTavern

Think of SillyTavern like a single room with a very forgetful assistant.

What happens every time you send a message in ST
💬
You type something
"Elena enters the archive looking for the Thornfield records"
↓
🧠
ST stuffs EVERYTHING into one giant message
Your character card + lorebook entries it found by keyword + the entire chat history + your message. Every. Single. Time.
↓
☁️
Sent to the AI as one enormous package
The AI reads the whole thing from scratch — it has no memory of last time. You're paying for all of it again.
↓
📖
You get a response
The AI wrote something, but it might have missed lore details because keyword matching is crude
Why this gets expensive and weird

Imagine hiring a writer who has amnesia. Every morning you have to re-read them your entire novel and all your world-building notes before they can write the next paragraph. That's what ST does — it "re-reads" everything on every single message. When your story is 100,000+ words, that's a lot of re-reading you're paying for.

Why lore gets missed

ST finds lore entries by searching for keywords in your message. If you write "Elena enters the archive" but your lorebook entry about the archive is tagged "Restricted Collection," ST won't find it. The AI then either skips the detail or makes something up.

Now, how the new system works

The New Way: Three Specialists Instead of One Forgetful Assistant

Instead of one overloaded room, think of this as a small creative studio with three people who each do one thing really well.

Meet the team
🎭
The Orchestrator
Your conversation partner. This is who you talk to. They have a personality, they remember what you've been discussing this session, and they figure out what you need. Think of them as a creative director — they don't do all the work themselves, they know who to ask.
⬇️ asks when needed ⬇️
📚
The Librarian
The lore expert. Has read every single one of your world-building files and remembers all of it. When anyone needs a fact — character details, location descriptions, history — they ask the Librarian. Never makes things up; if it's not in the files, they say so.
✍️
The Prose Writer
The actual author. When you want a scene written, this is who writes it. Before writing, they automatically check with the Librarian for any details they need. You never have to tell them to look things up — they just do it.

What happens when you ask for a scene:

Example: "Write Elena confronting the Archivist about the Thornfield records"
💬
You describe what you want
"Write the confrontation in the archive. Elena has the copied documents as proof."
↓
🎭

Hands the request to the Prose Writer, along with what's happened recently in the story
↓
✍️
Writer starts planning the scene
"I need to know about Elena, the Archivist, and the Thornfield records before I write..."
↓ asks the Librarian (automatically) ↓
📚

"Elena is a former apprentice, dark hair, dry humor. The Archivist is Aldric Senn, believes certain truths would destabilize the city. The Thornfield records are in the Restricted Collection..."

Using the real lore, matching the established story tone, with the right character voices

Added to the end of your story draft. Nothing is overwritten or lost.

Where your stuff lives

Your Files: Two Folders, That's It

Everything about your world and your story lives in simple text files on your computer. No database, no cloud storage, no special formats.

Your content folder
📁
narrative-content/
This is your folder. Everything creative lives here.
📚
lore/
Your world-building files. Characters, locations, factions, history — each one is a text file you can open and edit in any text editor. The Librarian reads all of these.
📖
story/
Your actual story draft. New scenes are added to the end. Nothing is ever deleted — the file only grows.
🔧
code-requests/
If the system identifies something that needs a code fix, it writes a request here. Patch picks these up and handles the technical side.
You can edit lore files directly

Want to change a character detail? Open the file, edit it, save. Next time you start the system, it picks up the changes. You can also ask the Orchestrator to make edits for you — "change Elena's hair color to black" and it'll update the file.

What the Orchestrator can do for you

It's More Than a Chatbot

The Orchestrator isn't just answering questions — it has tools. Think of it like talking to someone who has a desk, a filing cabinet, and a phone.

📚 Look up lore
"What do we know about the Thornfield family?"
✍️ Write scenes
"Write Elena discovering the hidden passage"
📝 Edit your files
"Update Elena's file — she now knows about the tunnels"
🔍 Search everything
"Find every mention of the Restricted Collection"
🎯 Answer technical questions
"How do I back up my story files?" (drops the personality, answers straight)
🔧 Request code changes
"The chapter splitting isn't working right" → writes a fix request for Patch

You just talk naturally. The system figures out which tool to use.

New feature: knowing how full your context is

The Context Meter

One problem with SillyTavern is that you can't easily tell when you're running out of room. The AI has a limited amount it can "think about" at once (called the context window). When you hit the limit, it starts forgetting the oldest parts of your conversation.
